## Target Audience

### Primary Segments
1. **Casual Collectors** - People with modest collections (10-100 items) who want to organize and track value
2. **Serious Hobbyists** - Enthusiasts with large, valuable collections needing professional organization
3. **Estate Planners** - People cataloging inherited collections for insurance/estate purposes

### Demographics
- Age: 18-55, skewing 25-45
- Income: Middle to upper-middle class
- Tech-savvy: Comfortable with mobile apps
- Passionate: Deep emotional connection to their collections

## User Pain Points

1. **"I have too many items to keep track of"** - Need digital organization
2. **"I don't know what my collection is worth"** - Value tracking
3. **"I can't find anything when I need to"** - Search/filtering
4. **"I want to show off my collection"** - Visual cataloging
5. **"I need to insure my collection"** - Documentation for insurance
6. **"My collection is spread across multiple places"** - Centralized tracking

## Market Trends

1. **Digital Organization** - Younger collectors expect apps, not spreadsheets
2. **Value Tracking** - YouTube/Instagram collectors documenting worth
3. **Mobile-First** - Snap and catalog on-the-go at shows/flea markets
4. **Social Sharing** - Show off collections on social media
5. **AI Integration** - Auto-identification becoming expected

## Opportunity

Most collection apps are either:
- Simple checklists (too basic)
- Niche-specific (trading cards only)
- Expensive desktop software (not mobile-friendly)
- Cluttered/ugly (not premium feel)

**VaultCollect fills the gap:** Beautiful, mobile-first, cross-category collection management at an accessible price point.
